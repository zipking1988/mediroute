# MediRoute — Pain Points (User Research)

## Core Pain Points Tourists Face

### 1. Don't Know to Call 119 (Biggest Issue)

This is the #1 systemic failure. Tourists — especially Americans — are conditioned to avoid calling ambulances because in their home countries it costs $600–$1,000+. In Japan, ambulance transport is completely free. As a result, they walk into hospitals, get turned away repeatedly, and end up in panic spirals. One tourist with a severe neck injury was turned away from 5 emergency departments simply because he walked in instead of calling 119.

**Critical insight**: hospitals are obligated to accept ambulance patients but can freely refuse walk-ins. The ambulance crew also pre-calls hospitals to find one with capacity — something a tourist cannot do alone.

### 2. Language Barrier — Forms, Symptoms, Prescriptions
- Nearly all clinics and hospitals operate in Japanese only
- Tourists can't fill out intake forms, describe symptoms precisely, or understand prescriptions
- Translation errors cause panic — one tourist was quoted ¥300,000 (~$2,100) when it was likely ¥30,000 — a 10x misread
- Hotel front desks are the most reliable bridge (they speak Japanese + know local clinics), but tourists don't know to use them for medical situations

### 3. System Confusion — Clinic vs. Hospital vs. ER
- **Clinics** handle non-emergencies but have very limited hours (often 8am–12pm, then 4pm–evening only)
- **Hospitals** need a referral letter from a clinic for non-emergencies — tourists who walk in without one get charged an extra ¥5,000–¥11,000 surcharge or are turned away entirely
- On **holidays/nights**, the only viable option is calling 119, but tourists don't know this

### 4. Fear-Driven Delay in Seeking Care
- Post titles like "I'm sick in Japan 😭", "Help! IMMEDIATE medical assistance", "4th Day in Tokyo and I'm Very Sick" reveal real psychological panic
- Delay caused by fear of costs or language barriers can turn manageable conditions (UTI, food poisoning, dehydration) into serious hospitalizations

### 5. Insurance Confusion
- Most tourists don't know travel insurance pays after the fact — you pay upfront, then claim reimbursement
- Many don't know their credit card may already include travel insurance
- Short-term Japan travel insurance is as low as ¥1,620 for 3 days — but tourists don't buy it because they don't think they'll need it

## Cost Reality (No Insurance)
| Situation | Cost |
|-----------|------|
| Ambulance ride | FREE |
| Clinic visit + meds | ¥5,000–¥15,000 (~$35–$105) |
| ER (labs + CT + meds) | ¥36,000–¥59,000 (~$250–$410) |
| Broken bone (full treatment) | ~$150–$600 USD |

Japan's costs are a fraction of US/Western prices — but tourists don't know this and avoid care because of fear.
